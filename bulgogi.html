Bulgogi

Description

Bulgogi (Korean: 불고기; /bʊlˈɡoʊɡiː/ bool-GOH-gee;[2] from Korean bul-gogi [pul.ɡo.ɡi]), literally "fire meat") is a gui (Korean-style grilled or roasted dish) made of thin, marinated slices of meat, most commonly beef, grilled on a barbecue or on a stove-top griddle. It is also often stir-fried in a pan in home cooking. Sirloin, rib eye or brisket are frequently used cuts of beef for the dish. The dish originated from northern areas of the Korean Peninsula, but is a very popular dish in South Korea, where it can be found anywhere from upscale restaurants to local supermarkets as pan-ready kits

Source: Wikipedia

Ingredients

  • Steak
  • Soy Sauce
  • Sugar
  • Green Onion and Garlic
  • Sesame seeds
  • Sesame oil
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Marinate the Steak
  2. Combine the rest of the ingredients and whisk until blended. Pour the marinade over the steak
  3. Allow the beef to marinate in the fridge for about an hour
  4. Grill the steak
  5. Grill the steak for about 1 to 2 minutes on each side
